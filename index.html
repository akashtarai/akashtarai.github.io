Biography

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the Department of Physical Sciences, IISER Berhampur, India with a Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Hyderabad, India.

My doctoral research focused on the fundamental studies of laser-produced plasma, with particular emphasis on its temporal characterization and elemental analysis using Laser-Induced Breakdown Spectroscopy (LIBS). A key contribution of my Ph.D. work was the development of a simplified LIBS intensity-ratio technique (SLICE) for rapid and accurate elemental quantification. Additionally, I was involved in the design and development of compact LIBS and Raman spectroscopy platforms integrated with machine learning algorithms for real-time classification and quantification of a wide spectrum of materials, including metals, polymers, biological specimens, and high-energy materials.

In my current postdoctoral role, I am working on wavefront sensing using Shack–Hartmann Wavefront Sensors (SHWS) for ophthalmic applications. My work involves developing advanced deep learning models to assess ocular aberrations associated with visual disorders such as cataract and myopia, with a focus on enhancing the speed and accuracy of diagnosis. This project has significantly deepened my expertise in computational imaging, deep neural network-based model development, and large-scale image data analysis.

My research interests lie at the intersection of experimental optics and artificial intelligence, with a strong emphasis on developing real-time diagnostic solutions for spectroscopic and optical sensing applications. I have extensive hands-on experience with high-power lasers, precision optical diagnostics, spectroscopic instrumentation, and AI-driven data analysis. I am passionate about translational research, interdisciplinary collaboration, and the development of intelligent, field-deployable systems for scientific and societal impact.
